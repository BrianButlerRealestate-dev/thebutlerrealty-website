Neighborhood Spotlights

Find the Area That Fits Your Life

The East Valley isn't one market — it's seven distinct communities. Here's an insider's look at each one.

Gilbert

"Where Families Thrive and Foodies Feast"

Top-rated schools, vibrant Heritage District dining scene, and some of the safest neighborhoods in the Phoenix metro.

Price Range$380K–$600K+
Best ForFamilies, young professionals

Mesa

"Diverse, Affordable, and Full of Hidden Gems"

The East Valley's largest city with the most diverse inventory. Often the most affordable entry point for first-time buyers.

Price Range$280K–$480K+
Best ForFirst-time buyers, budget-conscious

Chandler

"Tech Hub with Small-Town Charm"

Major tech employers, walkable downtown, excellent Chandler Unified schools, and a thriving restaurant scene.

Price Range$350K–$550K+
Best ForTech professionals, families

San Tan Valley

"Space, Value, and Room to Grow"

Newer homes, larger lots, and significantly lower price points. One of the fastest-growing communities in Arizona.

Price Range$300K–$450K+
Best ForFamilies wanting more space
Full neighborhood guide →

Queen Creek

"New Builds and Wide-Open Skies"

Agricultural charm meets modern living. Excellent new construction options and a strong family-oriented culture.

Price Range$380K–$550K+
Best ForNew construction seekers

Apache Junction

"Affordable Living with Mountain Views"

Superstition Mountain backdrop with the most affordable mountain-proximity living in the East Valley.

Price Range$250K–$400K+
Best ForOutdoor enthusiasts, budget buyers

Florence

"Maximum Space, Small-Town Living"

The most space per dollar in the East Valley. Large lots, newer construction, and a quiet small-town atmosphere.

Price Range$220K–$350K+
Best ForRemote workers, space seekers
